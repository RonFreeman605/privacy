Subject: Remember Me

Paul Steve, October 09, 1947.

Reply Forward

I'm sure you want to know who I am. I am a historian researching the background of the the India Pakistan conflict. I have written below the history of the conflict as I know it but remember: It's a conflict, there is always another side. This chain of emails is from a variety of people who all want to teach you about the ongoing problems between India and Pakistan.

In August 1947, India and Pakistanis alike woke up to twin countries without British rule. After many years of fighting, they were free of the British colonial government. However, the peace that they had fought to achieve would not last. In October of 1947, India and Pakistan declared war on each other. Why? The British separation line ignored tribal and cultural lines creating a Hindu minority in Pakistan and a Muslim minority in India. Religious violence broke out and had been continuing and India and Pakistan were gearing for war. The spark that started that war was the invasion of the Kashmir region of India by armed Pakistani tribesmen. India responded in Kashmir aid and launched a months long war. Sadly the ceasefire brokered in June 1, 1947 would not last. India and Pakistan have fought a series of wars since then each resulting in another ceasefire after ceasefire. India was the first of the two to win a nuclear arms race, and Pakistan soon followed. Thus we have reached the present day: two countries utterly opposed each armed with weapons of mass destruction.

Best Regards,
Paul Steve
