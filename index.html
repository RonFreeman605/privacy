I'm sure you love the new smart home devices. You know, the Nest Smart Home, the Alexa and the many other devices that you put in your home to make sure that it is safe and to make life more convenient. Have you ever wondered how do they work?

This short email chain will guide you through my journey as go through the two main privacy offenders: Smart Speakers and the Nest Smart Cameras. This email will provide a basic background explaining some key concepts and ideas. Make sure to read each email to the end as they all will explain how you can protect your privacy. When you feel you have finished, please press the "Trash" button get ready to protect your privacy. 

Before we can jump to smart speakers and cameras, I need to go into a little background about the internet. The internet initially began as a group of universities, attempting to share information to further scientific research. As the cost of a personal computer gradually decreased, a network of computers run by private individuals was formed. Websites were created by anyone who was able to use their computer to host them and the internet as we know it was born. A new business model was created with the start of social media platforms such as MySpace. A business model where the product was not a physical object, but you. Your life, your information sold to companies to better understand and target you. Many world governments recognized that this was becoming an issue and began to pass legislation.  The hallmark bill was the EU's: General Data Protection Regulation. A series of laws that made it illegal to collect information without explicit consent. Net Neutrality, became a world issue and companies outpaced governments.

The world of the 21st century is different than that of the 20th. Learning about the current privacy conflicts directly affect you. Education is the best defense, as only when we de not learn are we doomed to repeat.
